Anchor with a Rug

10 Quick Design Tips

From our Davanagere studio ‚Äî practical advice that makes a measurable difference.

01

Odd Numbers Rule

Always group decorative objects in sets of 3 or 5. Odd-numbered groupings feel more natural and dynamic than even pairs.

02

Curtains to The Ceiling

Hang curtain rods as close to the ceiling as possible ‚Äî it makes the room feel taller and more elegant, regardless of the actual ceiling height.

03

Mirror, Mirror

A large mirror on the wall opposite a window doubles the natural light and makes any room feel significantly larger.

04

60-30-10 Colour Rule

60% dominant (walls), 30% secondary (furniture), 10% accent (d√©cor). This formula creates balanced, cohesive rooms every time.

05

Plants Breathe Life

A fiddle-leaf fig, monstera, or snake plant adds height, colour, and a sense of warmth that no object can replicate. Start with one large statement plant.

06

Rug Size Matters

The most common mistake: too small a rug. Your rug should be large enough for at least the front legs of all seating pieces to sit on it.

07

Layer Your Textures

Mix wood, metal, glass, linen, and stone in every room. Textural variety is what makes rooms feel rich ‚Äî not expensive materials alone.

08

Edit Ruthlessly

Remove 30% of the objects from any shelf or surface. You'll almost always be surprised that it now looks better. Less is almost always more.

09

Focal Point First

Every room needs one focal point ‚Äî a fireplace, a large window, a statement artwork. Arrange the rest of the furniture to face or complement it.

10

Personalise Thoughtfully

Display things that mean something to you ‚Äî not just things that look good. Personal objects create warmth that no amount of money can buy.
